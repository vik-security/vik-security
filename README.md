## Hi 👋 I'm Vik

The content across repositories in vik-security is written from my own experience, research and lessons learned. Everything is written by me and not AI generated. You will also not find many biased opinions or harsh critisisms of certain approaches, tool and solutions. Instead, you will find references to industry approved and vouched-for standards and solutions, honest non-sponsored recommendations, as well as “Lessons learned” sections meant to outline mistakes and maybe prevent some headaches for you as a result. While I strongly encourage the use of AI as a tool for building knowlegde and helping strengthen projects and programs of all sort, I also believe cybersecurity, among many other things, requires human judgment, logic and accountability. 

Over the years I have worked in many technical roles that forced me to learn security the hard way - through implementation, troubleshooting and sometimes witnessing mistakes that lead to security breaches and data leaks. That hands-on experience ultimately shaped how I approach cybersecurity today. One of the skills I developed along the way is the ability to translate complex security controls and compliance requirements into simpler, practical explanations and real-world analogies. This helps both newcomers and experienced cyber professionals better understand how these controls actually function in an operational environment.

Early in my career I was eager to move quickly into leadership roles, but experience taught me something valuable - strong security leadership is built on a technical foundation. Hands-on experience is one of the most valuable teachers there is. My advice to anyone entering this field or advancing is simple — learn the systems and how they work individually and together, understand the risks, threats and vulnerabilities in each and most importantly, strive for quality and consistency of your work. 

The purpose of vik-security is to create a practical space where organizational security, governance, and compliance concepts are broken down into clear, understandable frameworks. The goal is not theory for the sake of theory, but practical security thinking designed to help reduce cyber risk in real environments.
